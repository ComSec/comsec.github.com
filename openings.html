Physical Layer and Cross Layer Techniques for Securing Wireless Communications

Description

  • A one-year postdoctoral research fellow position and a Ph.D. position are available within the Communications Security Lab, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Waterloo. The successful candidates are expected to conduct research on physical layer and cross layer techniques for securing wireless communications. Interested candidates that have a strong background in mathematics, cryptography and wireless communication systems are encouraged to apply.

Prerequisites for Postdoctoral Fellow Positions

  • An excellent Ph.D. degree in Electrical/Computer Engineering, Computer Science or a related area.
  • Strong publications focusing on communication system security at premium venues.
  • A strong background in mathematics, cryptography and wireless communication systems; programming and experimental skills are very welcome.

Prerequisites for Ph.D. Positions

  • An excellent Master's degree in Electrical/Computer Engineering, Computer Science or a related area.
  • A strong background in mathematics and wireless communication systems; a solid background in cryptography is an additional plus; programming and experimental skills are very welcome.

Procedure and Closing Date

  • Applicants are kindly requested to send their applications (a curriculum vitae, transcripts, research statement, names and contact addresses of references) by email in PDF-format to Dr. Xinxin Fan (x5fan at uwaterloo.ca).
  • Review of applications will begin immediately and will continue until the position is filled.
